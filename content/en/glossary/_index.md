---
title: "Gemological Glossary"
description: "Definitions of gemological terms used in the Bellerophon inclusion base"
---

## A

**Acicular** — Needle-shaped inclusion (e.g. rutile, tourmaline).

**Apatite** — Common phosphate mineral found as inclusions in many gemstones.

## B

**Birefringence** — The property of a crystal to split light into two rays. Visible through a loupe in some crystalline inclusions.

## C

**Cavity** — Empty or fluid-filled space trapped during crystal growth.

**Chromite** — Chromium-iron oxide, commonly included in garnets of ophiolitic origin.

## D

**Carbon dioxide (CO₂)** — Present as gas in some two-phase fluid inclusions.

## F

**Fingerprint** — Healed fluid inclusion forming a planar network resembling a fingerprint.

**Two-phase fluid** — Cavity containing both a liquid and a gaseous phase simultaneously.

## G

**Negative crystal** — A void within a crystal that mirrors the shape of the host mineral.

## H

**Hornblende** — Dark amphibole commonly included in garnets and pyroxenes.

## I

**Primary inclusion** — Formed simultaneously with the host crystal (synchro-crystallisation trap).

**Secondary inclusion** — Formed after crystallisation, along a healed fracture.

## N

**Negative crystal** — Cavity whose shape reflects the symmetry of the trapped mineral.

## P

**Photomicrography** — Photography through a microscope, here a gemological one.

## R

**Rutile** — Titanium dioxide (TiO₂), one of the most common and diagnostic inclusions in corundum.

## S

**Silk** — A cluster of fine rutile needles in corundum, visible at low magnification.

**Sphene (Titanite)** — Accessory mineral frequently included in Madagascar garnets.

## Z

**Zircon** — High-birefringence zirconium silicate; commonly included with pleochroic halos in corundum and garnet.
